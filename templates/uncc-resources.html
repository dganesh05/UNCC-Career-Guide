Frequently Asked Questions

How early should I start preparing for my job search? +

It's recommended to start preparing for your job search at least 6-9 months before graduation. This gives you time to develop your resume, portfolio, interview skills, and start networking. Many companies begin recruiting interns and entry-level positions well in advance, so early preparation gives you a competitive advantage.

How can I make my resume stand out to employers? +

To make your resume stand out, tailor it to each position you apply for by highlighting relevant skills and experiences. Use strong action verbs, quantify your achievements when possible, and ensure it's free of errors. Include relevant coursework, projects, and extracurricular activities if you have limited work experience. Keep the format clean and professional, and limit it to one page for entry-level positions.

What resources are available for students who are undecided about their career path? +

UNCC offers several resources for undecided students, including career assessments, one-on-one career counseling, major and career exploration workshops, and industry information sessions. You can also utilize the AI Career Coach tool, which provides personalized recommendations based on your interests and skills. Additionally, informational interviews with professionals and alumni can provide valuable insights into different career paths.

How can I best prepare for virtual interviews? +

To prepare for virtual interviews, ensure your technology is working properly beforehand and have a backup plan if technical issues occur. Choose a quiet, well-lit location with a professional background. Dress professionally as you would for an in-person interview. Practice looking at the camera, not the screen, to maintain eye contact. Use the AI Mock Interview tool to practice answering common questions and receiving feedback on your delivery, body language, and response content.
